### Why PyPSA?

PyPSA (Python for Power System Analysis) has rapidly become one of the most widely used open-source tools for modern energy system modeling. It enables detailed simulations of electricity networks, renewable energy integration, storage systems, grid expansion, and sector coupling within a transparent, flexible, and Python-based environment. Its reproducibility, scalability, and openness make it particularly well suited for academic research, utility planning, and national-level energy system studies.

What sets PyPSA apart is not only its technical versatility but also its widespread adoption across global institutions. It is used by international organizations such as the **International Energy Agency (IEA)**, the **Joint Research Centre (JRC) of the European Commission**, and the **Agency for the Cooperation of Energy Regulators (ACER)**. In India, PyPSA features in state government energy reports and studies by institutions such as **The Energy and Resources Institute (TERI)**. It is also used by major energy think tanks like **Ember** and in industry-led modeling projects by companies including **Saudi Aramco** and **Shell**.

The academic adoption of PyPSA is equally strong. A continuously growing list of institutional users is maintained in the official PyPSA documentation available on [official PyPSA documentation](https://pypsa.readthedocs.io/en/latest/references/users.html).

Beyond its technical capabilities and academic reach, PyPSA has also become increasingly relevant for **energy-sector careers worldwide**. Proficiency in PyPSA aligns closely with the skills required for roles in energy system analysis, grid planning, renewable integration, policy modeling, and energy consulting. This combination of methodological transparency, real-world relevance, and strong institutional adoption makes PyPSA particularly well suited for structured university courses as well as professional training and applied energy studies.


### Why _The PyPSA Handbook_?
As PyPSAâ€™s adoption expanded across academia, industry, and policy institutions, so did the demand for a clear and structured learning resource. While online documentation and community examples are valuable, they are often fragmented or tailored to narrow use cases. This made it difficult for new users to develop a systematic understanding, and for experienced users to rely on a single, stable reference.

The PyPSA Handbook was developed to address this gap. It is intended as both a **reference book** and a **learning resource**, supporting students, researchers, system planners, and professionals working in energy modeling and planning. The emphasis is on clarity, reproducibility, and practical relevance, allowing readers to move from basic concepts to advanced system studies with confidence.

Importantly, the book is written with **formal teaching and training environments in mind**, making it suitable for structured university courses as well as professional upskilling.

### Whatâ€™s Inside?

The book begins with the fundamentals: installing PyPSA, understanding its architecture, and modeling simple power networks. It then progresses step by step into more advanced topics such as dispatch and investment optimization, renewable and storage integration, long-term scenario analysis, and system reliability assessment.

Each chapter is designed to be **self-contained**, while also contributing to a broader modeling workflow. Hands-on examples, complete case studies, and real datasets are included throughout, allowing readers to directly run, adapt, and extend the models for their own applications. This structure makes the book equally suitable for semester-long courses, focused training modules, and independent study.

### For University Courses and Teaching

The PyPSA Handbook is explicitly designed to support **graduate-level university courses** in power systems, renewable energy modeling, and energy system planning. The chapter structure, worked examples, and reproducible code make it well suited for use in Bachelor's to PhD programs, as well as advanced elective courses.

Instructors can use the book to structure lectures, develop assignments, and design term projects based on realistic modeling problems. The progression from basic models to full system studies allows students to build both conceptual understanding and practical skills over the duration of a course.

### Relevance for the Energy Job Market

Beyond academia, the skills developed through this book are directly aligned with the requirements of the global energy job market. PyPSA is increasingly used in roles related to energy system analysis, grid planning, renewable integration, policy evaluation, and consulting.

By working through the models and case studies in the handbook, readers gain hands-on experience with workflows that mirror real-world energy planning tasks. This makes the book particularly relevant for students and professionals preparing for careers in energy modeling, system planning, and data-driven decision support across utilities, think tanks, consultancies, and research organizations.
